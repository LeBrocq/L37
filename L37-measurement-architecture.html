What the map reveals

We are rich exactly where it is cheapest to be rich: T3 activity telemetry at the top of the wheel. Sessions, engagement and social are well instrumented.

We are thin exactly where decisions get made. T2 between-stage conversion by source is mostly unbuilt, so we cannot yet answer whether we are attracting good-quality MQLs or which channels earn more budget. T1 is almost hollow: no blended CAC, no defensible pipeline contribution, no flywheel velocity.

The return spring is the sharpest gap. Advocacy is the largest historical driver of pipeline and it is the least instrumented part of the wheel. Referral rate and advocate-sourced pipeline are not measured at all. That is the single highest-value build.

Priority builds — in dependency order

  1. Source-tagged conversion & velocity: MQL→SQL→CDA
    One build unlocks three things: the MQL-quality read, the T2 mix decision, and the inputs for T1 leak and CAC.
  2. Advocacy return-spring
    Advocate-sourced pipeline and referral rate, derived from clients and NPS. Instruments the wheel's biggest driver.
  3. Blended CAC & trajectory
    The headline the Board actually allocates on. Depends on build 1 for clean source cost.
  4. Elevate AEO presence, demote total sessions
    Make answer-share the Be Found lead; move raw sessions to context.
  5. Fix HubSpot attribution integrity
    Precondition for trusting builds 1 to 3. Resolve Display Home miscredit and the Offline bucket.
