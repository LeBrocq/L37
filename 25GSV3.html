The Place

Most people drive straight past Mt Eliza. That's part of its appeal.

On summer weekends the Peninsula fills with people heading further south, to Mornington, Sorrento and Portsea. Mt Eliza rests quietly outside the influx of visitors. You can still find a park in the village, still get a table, still walk onto a beach and find almost nobody there but the locals. They tend to prefer it that way.

It is a genuine village. Familiar faces at the butcher, familiar parents at Saturday sport, children walking to school with friends. There are extraordinary homes here, many concealed behind established foliage. People come for space, privacy and permanence, not for visibility.
