Your journey with us

Building a home is a journey - and we walk it with you.

Most designers and builders show you their process. We share ours alongside the journey we know you're on, detailing what you're likely experiencing, and what we're doing behind the scenes to make you feel like you're not on your own.

Read it top to bottom, or jump to the stage that's on your mind.

i
Stage one

Exploring

The earliest stirrings of a custom home - curiosity, conversations, gathering ideas.

Typically 1–6 months
Some clients move quickly; most take their time here, and that's exactly right.
What you're experiencing

This is the dreaming phase - and it should feel that way. You're collecting ideas, noticing what's being built in your neighbourhood, and talking with family and friends about what's actually possible. There's real excitement here, but also a subtle weight: you want to imagine generously without setting yourself up for disappointment, and you're listening carefully to people who've done this before, looking for the stories that ring true.

What you need most right now is room to think, honest information, and a sense of what's realistic - not a sales pitch.

behind the scenes
What we're doing

You won't be in touch with us yet, but we're laying the groundwork for the conversation we'll eventually have. Our website, recent project stories, and open homes are designed to give you a clear, honest sense of who we are and how we work, so when you're ready, you're not starting from scratch.

  • Open and discoverable Knowledge Hub indepth guides, designer stories, explainer pages and helpful tools that show our work, our thinking, and what is actually required to build at this level are all available at L37.com.au.
  • Open homes Opportunities to walk through real Latitude 37 homes and feel the craft in person, not just in renders. You could also have a conversation with the people who live there.
  • Creating space for exploration If you sign up for updates, we share Open Home schedules and considered content - never pressure - so you can keep exploring at your own pace.
A note from us
If a designer or builder tells you "anything is possible" at this stage, treat it cautiously. We'd rather help you understand what's likely, what's stretch, and what isn't - even before you've engaged us.
ii
Stage two

Planning

Narrowing the field - meeting designers and builders, testing fit, understanding cost.

Typically 2–4 months
Can happen faster if you're ready to move; we'll meet you at your pace.
What you're experiencing

The mood shifts. You're hopeful but more careful now - shortlisting, meeting designers and builders, comparing quality and reputation, weighing whether to engage a designer separately or work with one partner. You want clarity on cost without being talked down to, and you're judging trust as much as competence: who feels safe, who feels straight with you, who has the chemistry to walk this journey with you for the next two or three years.

You need feasibility, options, and a real sense of what your money will buy...without the horror stories playing in the background.

behind the scenes
What we're doing

If we meet at this stage, our job is to be useful, not to win you. We'll have an open conversation about your land, your vision, and your budget, and tell you honestly where the fit is strong and where it isn't. The goal is to help you make a good decision, even if that decision is to take more time.

  • Introductory conversation An unhurried, no-pressure meeting to understand what you're trying to build and what success looks like for you.
  • Honest feasibility A grounded conversation about what's realistic at your budget - not a sales pitch about what's possible in theory.
  • Onboarding materials Clear written information about how we work, what to expect, and what each stage costs, so nothing is hidden.
A note from us
Mis-aligned expectations are the single biggest source of friction in custom design and builds. We'd rather have a hard conversation now than a harder one later.
iii
Stage three

Choosing

Testing your preferred partner with a real concept - the first true measure of fit.

Typically 6–10 weeks
From design brief to concept presentation. Pace depends on how much refinement you want.
What you're experiencing

This is the moment of focused investment. You've sat in the design brief meeting, and you're watching closely: are they listening, do they understand us, can we afford what we actually want? You're deepening your understanding of how things work and testing the chemistry. You want certainty before you go further, and you want to feel seen and heard, not processed.

What you need is genuine listening, an honest reality-check on cost, and a concept that reflects you, not a template with your name on it.

behind the scenes
What we're doing

This is Stage 1 of our Concept Design process. A small fee secures site information and gets us moving. Our Head of Client Experience and Senior Designer work with you to translate your brief into a first concept - with options - and an indicative build cost, so you can test the partnership before committing further.

  • Discovery session An initial project discussion to understand your site, your brief, and your priorities.
  • Custom Home Design Agreement A clear, written agreement covering Stage 1 (Concept Design). Investment: $1,000 plus site information costs.
  • Design brief meeting A detailed conversation between you, your Client Experience Lead, and your Designer about your brief and project value.
  • Design presentation meeting We present the first design concepts alongside an indicative build cost, and we discuss it openly.
A note from us
When the numbers feel tight, we don't frame it as "taking scope out" - that makes refinement feel like loss. We frame it as shaping the brief together, so what stays is what matters most.
iv
Stage four

Designing

Concept becomes a considered design - trade-offs made, decisions confirmed, vision refined.

Typically 8–14 weeks
Stage 2 and Stage 3 combined. We can move faster if you're decisive, slower if you want to sit with choices.
What you're experiencing

Excitement returns - your home is becoming real on the page. But it comes with its own weight: hard decisions about design, materials, and function; trade-offs between vision and budget; the desire for early proof that you've chosen well. You're refining details with our guidance, sharing progress with people you love, and watching for the signals that say you're in safe hands.

What you need now is collaborative shaping, calm decision-making, and the reassurance that the choices in front of you are the right ones.

behind the scenes
What we're doing

This is Stage 2 (Design Development) and Stage 3 (Design Approval) of our Concept Design process. We refine your design, build a full-colour 3D model, and prepare a Custom Home Proposal - a detailed cost breakdown that makes the next decision clear-eyed.

  • Stage 2 — Design Development Concept amended and finalised; development of a full-colour 3D model so you can see your home in dimension, not just in plan.
  • Preliminary pricing The Custom Home Proposal (CHP) is developed and presented, outlining the build cost breakdown line by line.
  • Stage 3 - Design Approval Investment: 2.5% of CHP value. Your sign-off to move into full project documentation.
A note from us
Trade-off conversations should feel like shaping, not signing in blood. We sit on the same side of the table as you when we work through them.
v
Stage five

Committing

Working drawings, interior design, contract - the partnership becomes formal.

Typically 4–6 months
Project documentation, interior design and contract preparation often run in parallel.
What you're experiencing

You're engaged and energised at the start - there's a lot happening. As detail compounds, decision fatigue can creep in: working drawings, fittings, fixtures, appliances, lift, pool, landscape. You want to feel confident that each choice is the right one and that the partnership has the steadiness to carry you through. You're reviewing the contract carefully, sharing the design with people who matter to you, and preparing to meet the construction team who will build your home.

What you need is steady pacing, real collaboration, and pricing clarity at every step.

behind the scenes
What we're doing

This is our Project Documentation phase, running in parallel with Interior Design and culminating in contract signing and construction preparation. A lot of disciplines coordinate here - design, engineering, interior design, building surveying, estimating - and our job is to make that complexity feel straight forward from your perspective.

  • Working drawings Town planning (if required), heating & cooling, electrical, energy rating, engineering - all coordinated and documented.
  • Interior design Interior Design Brief Meeting, fitting & fixture selections, presentation of material palette and finishes, detailed schedule.
  • QC review & contract Project documentation goes through Latitude 37 QC review. Bill of Quantities reconciles allowances. Contract is prepared and signed (5% deposit, less previous payments).
  • Construction preparation Demolition (where applicable), insurances, service approvals, building permits. The site file is issued, ready for construction to start.
A note from us
Interior pricing is the easiest place to lose trust. We hold ourselves to transparency here - clear allowances, clear reconciliation, no surprises at Bill of Quantities.
vi
Stage six

Building

Your home rises - base, frame, lockup, fix, completion. Visible progress and just enough detail.

Typically 12–18 months
Varies with complexity, site conditions, and the scale of your home.
What you're experiencing

The excitement of seeing your design come to life is real, and so is the impatience. You're tracking progress against the plan, sharing reports and photos with family and friends, and making progressive payments along the way. On the good weeks, progress is visible. On the quieter weeks - when trades coordinate, materials arrive, inspections happen - it can feel like nothing's moving, even when it is. You want reassurance that the quality matches the promise, and steady communication when timelines shift.

What you need is honest progress updates, a clear point of contact, and confidence that the craft on site matches the craft on paper.

behind the scenes
What we're doing

Construction runs through five stages, each with its own QA inspection and site meetings. Your Site Manager and Construction Manager carry the day-to-day, with independent Quality Inspectors at every gate so quality is never just our word for it.

  • Base Site set up, underground services, piers and piles (if applicable), formwork, concrete slab - Base QA inspection complete.
  • Frame Frame and structural steel erected; Frame QA inspection and Building Surveyor inspection; Frame Meeting on site with you.
  • Lockup Windows, roofing, HVAC and plumbing roughed in, cladding completed, external doors installed. Your home is now lockable.
  • Fix Electrical roughed in, pre-plaster Hold Point QA, plastering, joinery, skirting, architraves and internal doors. Pre-paint QA.
  • Completion Painting, tiling, fit-offs, timber flooring, cleaning, caulking. Building Surveyor final inspection. Certificate of Occupancy issued.
A note from us
"Days when nothing happened" are part of every build - but they shouldn't feel that way to you. We commit to regular, honest updates so the quiet weeks feel transparent, not invisible.
vii
Stage seven

Living

Handover, moving in, and the long tail of maintenance, warranty, and relationship.

Handover + 12 months of warranty
Maintenance list within 90 days; check-ins at 3, 6 and 12 months.
What you're experiencing

You walk through your finished home, accept the keys, and move in. There's a deep pride here, especially when the experience has held up to the promise - and a strong hope that the relationship continues with the same care. You're reflecting on the journey, telling the story to friends and family, settling into a new chapter. Small issues may surface; you want them resolved without friction.

What you need is a clean handover, responsive aftercare, and the sense that Latitude 37 stands behind the home for the long run, not just up until the keys change hands.

behind the scenes
What we're doing

Handover is staged with care - pre-completion QA, client final inspection, outstanding works list, final cleaning. After handover, our Warranty Manager and team stay in touch through structured check-ins, so the home keeps performing the way it should.

  • Handover PCQA inspection, defects completed, re-cleaning, client final inspection, Outstanding Works List, final QA sign-off, handover site meeting.
  • Maintenance & warranty Maintenance list submitted within 90 days, Warranty Manager inspection, agreed defects completed within 28 days.
  • Ongoing check-ins Latitude 37 team check-ins at 3, 6 and 12 months - staying close to how the home is living.
A note from us
How a journey ends shapes how it's remembered. We pay particular attention to handover and aftercare for that reason - the quality of the last chapter matters as much as the first.

Process exists to deliver experience.

Every step we've shown you is in service of what you're feeling at each stage - clarity when you need to decide, space when you need to think, honesty when the conversation is hard. Client feedback on the way through helps us improve how you experience your journey.

That's what Latitude 37 cares about most...that we create experiences that you want to recommend.
