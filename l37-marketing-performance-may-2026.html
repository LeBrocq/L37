What matters this month

Discovery and consideration are both up strongly month on month. The constraint is not conversion - once a prospect is qualified, SQL to CDA is running at 75%. The constraint sits at both ends of the flywheel: too few qualified, well-matched enquiries coming in, and a referral engine that is quietly degrading.

Visibility is shifting

Sessions and engagement are up, but AI Overviews are compressing traditional web traffic, so visibility now has to be read beyond sessions. Early AEO signals are encouraging: L37 is the most cited brand in our tracked set, and two of the last six CDAs came via AI referral.

Conversion is healthy

SQL to CDA is strong at 75% YTD. The drag is upstream - readiness, fit and budget alignment below $1.5m - not closing. The fix is better qualification, not more raw volume.

The engine is at risk

Advocacy is the largest historical driver of pipeline and it is degrading. NPS response sits at 14%, almost certainly masking passive and detractor sentiment. A weak referral engine starves the whole flywheel.

Commentary

Discovery and familiarity are up overall month on month, despite continued structural pressure on traditional website traffic. Industry data points the same way - Forbes reports a 61% reduction in organic click-through when an AI Overview is present. Visibility now has to be assessed beyond website sessions alone.

Paid search remains the strongest driver of web traffic, so we have launched seven tightly targeted campaigns aligned to high-intent services, locations and search behaviour. Over the next six months we expect a gradual reduction in reliance on traditional SEM, alongside increased testing of AEM - paid visibility within AI-led discovery.

Following the Knowledge Hub launch, the focus is brand visibility across AI platforms, in both answers and citations. AEO visibility, citation frequency and share of voice tracking has just started, so the near-term job is to set benchmarks and report movement and competitive visibility over time.

Commentary · greatest concern

As the largest historical driver of the sales pipeline, advocacy is the greatest concern in the flywheel. The infrastructure is now in place, but response volume is low: 6 completed responses from 44 surveys issued (14%), and only 3 with verbatim comments. Low response rates can create a misleadingly positive view of client experience.

All six responses are from Promoters (9–10/10). These clients are clearly satisfied, but the set is too small and too positively skewed to represent the broader base. A 35–65% response rate would give a more reliable read of sentiment and emerging experience risk.

The key concern is that non-response may be masking passive or detractor sentiment. History supports taking this seriously: recorded detractors rose from zero to six between 2021 and 2024, and the available data is more likely to err optimistic than negative.

Immediate opportunity: proactively review clients nearing completion and resolve any unmet needs or concerns before handover. This has to go beyond a positive handover moment - a happy handover will not overcome genuine dissatisfaction if important needs have gone unresolved through the journey.
