1.2

Two moments that shaped the business

The philosophy of Latitude 37 was shaped early. Not through strategy documents, but through real conversations with clients.

Val and Bob

One of our earlier clients was a couple, Val and Bob.

Val and her husband were retired and avid golfers. They loved the home Latitude 37 designed and built for them - a single storey golf-front home in Sandhurst. So much so that they offered to open their home to prospective clients.

The L37 team would arrive with guests, wait outside as Val invited them in, made tea and showed them around their new home, sharing their experience of building with Latitude 37. No questions were off the table. A full, frank and genuine endorsement of our service and reliability.

But it didn't stop there. They shared their stories with friends at the golf club, and new introductions came in thick and fast.

When someone independent proudly shares their experience, others listen.

That is how Latitude 37 grew. Over the next ten to twelve years, Latitude 37 built upwards of 80 homes in Sandhurst.

Jodie and Darren

Doug was leaving Val and Bob's construction site one day when he met a young mother pushing a pram. Seeing the site signage, she asked if he was from Latitude 37. He said he was.

She introduced herself as Jodie and started talking about the site. Not about the house being built - but about how the site looked. Clean. Organised. Respectful of the street. She said it showed that we cared about our client's property.

She compared it with another builder down the road whose site was messy and disruptive to neighbours. For Jodie, this signified a lack of care and poor quality.

Doug continued to listen.

Jodie and her husband had already commissioned a design and asked if Latitude 37 would build it. At the time, Latitude 37 preferred to work on fully integrated design-and-build projects.

Instead of declining, Doug suggested reviewing and improving the design for a modest fee - refundable if they didn't find the recommendations valuable.

Jodie and Darren loved the improvements. Latitude 37 built their home. They remain friends to this day.

Listening carefully often leads to better work.

1.4

The category problem

The high-end custom home industry has become increasingly uniform.

Beautiful images. Minimalist language. Limited guidance.

Many brands focus on the finished product while leaving clients to navigate the complexity of the journey on their own.

The industry tends to split into two modes:

  • Architects prioritise design purity.
  • Builders prioritise construction efficiency.

Clients are left trying to bridge the gap between the two.

Latitude 37 operates in that space - combining the continuity and care of a close working relationship with the structure, capability and discipline to deliver consistently, at scale.

1.6

Who we design for

Not every client approaches a custom home in the same way.

The clients who create the best outcomes are what we call Confident Collaborators. They value design, but also clarity, partnership and expertise.

Within this group there are several distinct primary mindsets, understanding that many clients can hold more than one.

Vision-First Families

Creative and curious. They explore possibilities and want a home that reflects their identity.

Pragmatic Investors

Analytical and careful. They seek transparency, certainty and long-term value.

Focused on how life unfolds in the home - family routines, gatherings and everyday rituals.

1.8

Our promise

Building a home takes courage.

It is thrilling to imagine what life could look like in a new space. But committing to that vision can feel daunting.

  • Clients question whether the vision will hold.
  • Whether the process will overwhelm.
  • Whether the outcome will match what they imagined.

Latitude 37 responds in two ways:

  • We create the space for clients to explore what matters most to them.
  • We deliver with the discipline and precision required to bring it to life - and for it to endure.

The result is that every home carries two signatures: the family who imagined it, and the team who built it.

1.10

What this looks like in practice

"Latitude to create. Crafted for life." is not a statement. It's how we make decisions every day.

It shows up in small moments.

Latitude to create means

  • Taking the time to understand what matters before moving to solutions.
  • Creating space for clients to explore, not rushing them to decide.
  • Staying open to better ideas, even when they challenge the brief.
  • Being curious, not defensive, when something doesn't feel right.

Crafted for life means

  • Getting the details right, even when no one is watching.
  • Thinking beyond handover - how the home will live, age and endure.
  • Following through on commitments, not leaving loose ends.
  • Making decisions that hold up over time, not just in the moment.

Often, these two are in tension. Speed vs care. Creativity vs buildability. Client ambition vs budget reality.

Our role is not to take sides. It is to navigate that tension with judgement.

What it doesn't look like

  • Rushing decisions to keep things moving.
  • Defaulting to "we've always done it this way."
  • Passing issues on because they sit with another team.
  • Accepting "good enough" when better is achievable.
  • Avoiding hard conversations with clients or colleagues.
  • Prioritising speed or cost at the expense of the outcome.

How this shows up across the business

In early conversations - Exploring, Planning, Choosing

  • We don't jump straight to solutions.
  • We help clients make sense of what matters, even if it slows things down.
  • We guide, rather than sell.

In design and pre-site - Designing, Committing

  • We challenge ideas constructively to improve the outcome.
  • We resolve complexity early, not pass it forward.
  • We align design, cost and buildability - not treat them separately.

In construction - Building, Living

  • We don't walk past details that will be visible later.
  • We communicate early when something changes.
  • We finish properly, not just practically.

Across every role

  • We take responsibility for the whole experience, not just our part.
  • We share context early to avoid downstream issues.
  • We make decisions with the client in mind, not just the task in front of us.

This is what clients experience. Not our process, but how we show up within it.

3.2

Safety & responsibility

Creating a safe place to work is a non-negotiable at Latitude 37. This applies to our team, our subcontractors, our clients and the broader public. Every person who enters our sites must be protected from harm.

We maintain a zero-tolerance approach to unsafe practices - particularly in relation to working at heights and electrical safety, which remain the most significant risks in our industry.

All site staff must hold appropriate OHS qualifications, be trained in first aid and carry a first aid kit. Non-site staff and clients must be appropriately inducted and supervised at all times.

Our OHS systems and procedures (managed through BuildPass) must be followed on every project, without exception. This includes:

  • OHS Management System Manual
  • Project-specific SWMS for all high-risk work
  • RCD protection, tested monthly
  • Tagged tools and leads
  • Secure, well-maintained and clearly accessible sites
  • Appropriate fall protection for all work above 2 metres

Independent safety audits are conducted regularly. Any hazards identified must be addressed immediately.

Safety is not a checklist - it is a shared responsibility. If something is unsafe, we act.

Well-run, safe and organised sites also reflect the care we bring to our clients' homes. They are visible proof of how we work.

3.4

Construction: how we deliver

Construction is where intent becomes reality. It's where design, planning and client trust are tested - and proven - every day.

This requires consistent judgement, balancing design intent, delivery discipline and client expectations at every stage. The role of the Construction team is to deliver each home with care, precision, accountability and respect for the client's experience.

Client experience

We maintain a consistent, proactive relationship with every client.

  • Speak with every client weekly by phone, providing clarity on progress and what comes next
  • Follow up key points in writing where needed
  • Record all interactions in OSC
  • Ensure all formal meetings are documented and agreed

Clients should never feel uncertain about where they stand. If something changes, we communicate early.

Planning and delivery

Strong planning underpins confident delivery.

  • Plan at least 30 days ahead
  • Maintain momentum across all sites
  • Minimise delays by reducing gaps, not compressing quality
  • Coordinate trades proactively and realistically
  • Take ownership of delivery timelines, including during periods of leave

We don't wait for direction. We anticipate and plan ahead.

Quality

Details matter. Small oversights compound quickly and are visible to clients. We take responsibility for the complete outcome, not just our part.

  • Complete all required QC checks at each stage
  • Maintain BuildPass accurately and consistently
  • Address issues before work progresses
  • Ensure the home is watertight, compliant and ready before advancing stages

We don't pass problems forward. We resolve them.

Cost and commercial discipline

We balance value, cost and outcome - not just price.

  • Approve invoices only when work is complete and meets expectations
  • Ensure all work is properly authorised before proceeding
  • Manage contingency and spend responsibly

Every decision has a commercial impact.

Safety

Safety standards must be upheld at all times.

  • Follow all OHS requirements without exception
  • Conduct regular audits
  • Act immediately on any risks identified

We do not walk past unsafe conditions.

Variations and changes

Changes are a natural part of a custom home journey. We approach change with openness, but not casually. Our role is to manage them carefully - balancing client intent, buildability, timing and cost.

  • No changes proceed without proper documentation and client approval
  • All variations must be clearly scoped, priced and agreed before work begins
  • We guide clients through implications, ensuring informed decisions

Where requests introduce complexity or risk, we assess them carefully and involve the Construction Director where required. Well-managed changes protect the integrity of the build - and the client experience.

Ownership and continuity

Every project is shared, but never fragmented.

  • We take responsibility beyond our immediate role
  • We do not defer issues because they sit "with another team"
  • We maintain continuity of information, intent and decision-making

Clients experience the whole, not our individual responsibilities.
